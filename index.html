Who's frying

A donut worth the wait.

I'm Javaria — Jojo to most people. I started with a simple stubbornness: most donuts in this city are made fast and eaten faster. I wanted the opposite.

So every batch rests in the cold for more than twenty hours before it ever meets the oil. That's what gives brioche its pull-apart crumb and its lightness. I fry in small batches, the evening you order, and glaze while it's still warm.

This is the start of something bigger. Every box gets me a little closer to doing it full-time.
